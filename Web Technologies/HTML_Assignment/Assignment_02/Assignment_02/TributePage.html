Ratan Naval Tata

Ratan Tata is a renowned Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. Born on December 28, 1937, he is known for transforming the Tata Group into a global business conglomerate. His leadership style, ethical business practices, and vision for India’s industrial and social development have earned him great respect both in India and internationally.

Ratan Tata is admired not only for his business acumen but also for his humility and dedication to nation-building. Under his leadership, Tata Group expanded significantly in industries like steel, IT, automobiles, and hospitality.

Notable Achievements and Contributions:

  • Chairman of Tata Group from 1991 to 2012, and interim chairman in 2016–17.
  • Led major global acquisitions like Jaguar-Land Rover and Corus Steel.
  • Launched Tata Nano, the world’s cheapest car, to make cars accessible to all.
  • Awarded Padma Bhushan (2000) and Padma Vibhushan (2008) by the Government of India.
  • Established Tata Trusts, contributing to health, education, and rural development across India.
  • Known for philanthropy and humanitarian aid, including support during COVID-19 and natural disasters.
  • Actively invests in young Indian startups and entrepreneurs through RNT Associates.
